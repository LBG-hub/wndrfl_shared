# wndrfl Voice and Tone Guide
**Version 2.0**

---

## The Voice

wndrfl speaks with quiet confidence. Nature-connected. Aspirational but grounded. First person collective: "we," "our," "us." Never corporate. Never loud. Never sales-driven.

The writing feels like a conversation with someone who has been somewhere remarkable and wants to share it without overselling it.

---

## Core Principles

**Short declarative statements.** One idea per sentence. No hedging.

**Sensory and specific.** Not "beautiful scenery" — but "the moment the ridge drops away and there's nothing but ocean." Write what it feels like, not what it looks like in a brochure.

**Lowercase in expressive contexts — intentional.** The brand writes `wndrfl` in lowercase always. Sentence-level lowercase in headlines and captions is acceptable and preferred in expressive contexts.

**Poetic rhythm over journalistic structure.** Copy breathes. Paragraphs 2–4 lines maximum.

**No punctuation between the brand phrase and the logotype.** `The sea is wndrfl.` — the period belongs to the sentence, not to wndrfl.

---

## Copy Patterns

### Hero / Tagline Pattern
```
[Subject] is wndrfl.
```
Subject in Garamond upright. `wndrfl` in Garamond italic. No punctuation between phrase and logotype. Full stop at end of sentence, not after logotype.

Approved examples:
- Life is *wndrfl*.
- Our planet is *wndrfl*.
- The sea is *wndrfl*.
- Adventure is *wndrfl*.
- Silence is *wndrfl*.

The pattern works with any subject that aligns with wndrfl's ethos. Use it deliberately. Not for every piece of copy.

### Event Copy Pattern
Headline: event name in Garamond italic, centered.
Body: 2–3 lines maximum. Evocative and specific. GT Pressura Mono. No adjective pile-ups.
Date / Location: plain, direct. Day + Month + Year. Location name only.

### Partnership / Collaboration Copy
All elements left-aligned. Tone: collaborative, not hierarchical. The copy acknowledges both parties as equals.

### Two-Column Body Copy (Master Graphics)
Left column: evocative narrative — 3–5 lines, sensory, specific.
Right column: concise statement — 1–2 lines, declarative.
Separated by a thin ruled line. Space Mono. Small.

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Social posts | Evocative. Brief. One idea. |
| Event copy | Informational but atmospheric. Logistical details in service of anticipation. |
| Partnerships | Equal footing. Collaborative warmth without excess. |
| Email footer | Minimal. Name, role, wndrfl.com. No tagline. |
| Stationery | Invisible. The brand speaks through the design, not the words. |
| AI prompts | Precise and instructional. The voice guides are not applied to functional prompts. |

---

## Prohibited Language

Never use: leverage, synergy, disrupt, curate (as a verb), exclusive, elite, bespoke, premium, luxury, immersive (overused), transformative (overused), world-class, next-level, game-changing.

Never use exclamation points in primary copy.

Never use aggressive CTAs: "Buy now," "Don't miss out," "Limited spots."

Never use passive CTAs that imply the audience needs convincing.

---

## Language for Graphic Elements

All graphic elements are original commissioned art. When describing them in any context — asset catalogs, AI prompts, documentation, alt text, or instructions — use formal descriptive language only:

**Approved descriptors:** dot, circle, path, stroke, cluster, trail, line, band, column, organic form, asymmetric arrangement, layered composition, etching-style rendering, hand-drawn quality, varied density, irregular spacing, flowing parallel strokes.

**Never use:** tribal, ethnic, indigenous, folk, native, cultural, geographic origin language, heritage, artisanal (in a way that implies cultural origin), or any descriptor that assigns cultural or geographic provenance to the elements.

This is a standing constraint that applies to all output formats — documentation, AI prompts, alt text, catalog entries, Canva descriptions, and spoken/written references.

## URL and CTA

`wndrfl.com` — always lowercase. GT Pressura Mono. Very small. Bottom edge of composition. 40% opacity. No additional punctuation. No "visit" or "learn more."

## What wndrfl Does Not Sound Like

- A hotel booking site
- A wellness app
- A corporate sustainability report
- An NGO fundraising campaign
- A luxury travel magazine
- Anything that uses "journey" as a metaphor rather than a literal description of travel
